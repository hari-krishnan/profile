Detection-Gated Glottal Segmentation

A lightweight YOLOv8 + U-Net pipeline for glottal segmentation in high-speed videoendoscopy. A detection gate suppresses false positives during glottal closure and occlusion frames, enabling zero-shot transfer across clinical datasets without institutional fine-tuning.

Trained on 600 frames (GIRAFE), achieving DSC 0.81 on GIRAFE and DSC 0.85 on the large-scale BAGLS benchmark zero-shot. Validated on a 65-subject clinical cohort — coefficient of variation of glottal area was a significant marker for pathological vocal function (p=0.006). Runs at ~35 fps.

HSDI Frame Rate & Pixel Excursion Calculator

Faster cameras don't always mean better data. For periodic motion like vocal fold vibration, there is a bounded feasible interval of frame rates — too slow misses peaks, but too fast causes inter-frame displacement to drop below one pixel, collapsing velocity SNR.

This interactive tool computes that interval from your imaging setup: enter fundamental frequency, open/speed quotients, pixel excursion, and target frames-per-phase to get f_s_min, f_s_max, minimum required pixel excursion, and expected velocity SNR. Based on research validated across 67 subjects at 4000–16000 fps.

Companion to: "Optimal Sampling and Noise Mitigation for Vocal Fold Kinematics in High-Speed Digital Laryngeal Imaging" (manuscript under preparation)
